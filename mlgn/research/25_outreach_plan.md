# 25 — Outreach & networking plan (LinkedIn / email / conference)

_Compiled 2026-07-15 from a 26-agent research workflow (12 person-cluster profilers, 12
adversarial fact-checkers, 1 outreach-norms evidence sweep, 1 roster-completeness sweep).
All role/hiring claims below survived independent re-verification unless flagged.
Question answered: whom to connect with on LinkedIn about LGN research, when (pre- vs
post-P1), and whether bare adds are the right move. Goals: (a) PhD PI relationships,
(b) collaborations, (c) paper visibility._

## 0. The headline answers

1. **Do NOT send bare (no-message) connection requests before P1 is public.** Three
   independent reasons:
   - **Leak mechanics:** a connection request reveals your name + headline + affiliation
     to the recipient *regardless of acceptance*, and profile views notify by default.
     An add from "recurrent-LGN researcher, Luleå" into the DISCO/ISTA orbit is a
     pre-publication direction leak to the two labs racing you (documented LinkedIn
     product behavior; see §5).
   - **Conversion:** measured data (sales population, best available) shows a note does
     not change acceptance (~26% either way) but **~doubles post-accept reply rate** —
     bare adds produce silent connections, which is worth ~nothing for goals (a)/(b).
   - **PIs don't live there:** every faculty-authored advice source treats **email as
     the canonical prospective-PhD channel**; none endorse LinkedIn. LinkedIn is a
     secondary visibility layer for peers/industry, not the PI channel.
2. **The correct sequencing is artifact-first.** No direct A/B data exists, but all
   norms converge: for a solo researcher the arXiv link is the *only verifiable
   credential*, and it also dissolves the leak asymmetry. P1 hits arXiv ~Aug-Sep 2026 —
   that is the trigger for the main outreach wave, ~14 months before applications.
3. **A small "safe list" IS worth connecting with now** (§2): people outside the race
   where an early relationship compounds (imec, DWN/UT-Austin, FINN/EmLogic,
   Transcendent Logic, DiffLogic-CA first author, Vargas). Always with a short note
   (~150-300 chars) referencing *their published work only* — never the recurrent/
   gating/register direction.

> Subject: Recurrent logic gate networks — workshop paper building on {their paper}
> 1. One sentence: who you are + the artifact ("solo workshop paper, NeurIPS'26 WS, arXiv link").
> 2. Two sentences: the specific bridge to THEIR recent work (cite one 2025-26 paper concretely).
> 3. One sentence: small ask (15-min call / "would value your read" / interest in the group's directions).
> Follow up once after ~1 week. (Norms: specificity + artifact + small ask; generic emails are the #1 stated non-response reason.)
